In Memory of Pitzi the Cat

Always In My Mind, Forever in My Heart

"The comfort of having a friend may be taken away, but not that of having had one."

- Seneca, Roman Stoic Philosopher

Pitzi's Life:

Pitzi's date of birth is unknown. That is because she was brought to me already grown up (I think she was one or two years old at that time), at Christmas Time in December 2008, with the request to take care of her for one month period.

That was because she was kept in an office and all the people were about to leave for a few weeks for the winter vacation. Thus, Pitzi the Cat had no place to live in for that period of time and if she would have been let outside on her own, there were dogs that usually killed cats. Also, Pitzi the cat didn't know how to live in the streets, search for food and fend herself like a stray cat. So I accepted to take care of her for one month and the rest is history.

She proved to be Incredibly Loyal and Protective. Once, she thought my life was in danger (due to a silly joke of a woman that was a close friend) and even if Pitzi was way smaller than the "attacker" she fiercely intervened between us and stand up to defend me and save my life - she was so sweet and courageous and clearly communicated her feelings and intentions and we saw that she had no hesitation to put her life on the line for mine).

She was also Loving and Full of Affection and was always alert in order to protect the house and quick to Welcome me when returning home, showing how much she missed me. There was no need to ever tell her to do or not to do something, cos she always felt what I want and did that accordingly.

Pitzi was Incredibly Smart. I know that people who haven't known her would smile and think this is an exaggeration, but Pitzi was able to invent by her own a complex Language in order to communicate with me... that is an Incredible thing for a cat. She was also Very Curious about how the toilet and faucets work, she figured out on her own how to open a closed door by jumping on the door handle or press it with her front paws and did a lot of other amazing things.

She also possessed a Very Kind Heart which is a strange thing when talking about a top predator such as a cat. But that's the Truth, as she once found out a little sparrow who had not known yet how to fly and was hiding itself in a small hole in my yard. Pitzi stayed by the little bird guarding it for 10 to 15 minutes at least, until I understood that she found something and won't leave it till she would gain my attention in order to come and solve the problem - which I did... I took the little sparrow and went with it to the vet, who told me they will keep it and feed it till it will be able to fly, cos it is very little and I will not be able to take care of it on my own.

Pitzi proved again and again to be a True Friend! On 30 October 2018 I was about to die in a motorcycle accident in which I was thrown with 50km/h head first into a fixed object because of a car driver who carelessly changed lanes, hit me and run away even if some witnesses tried to stop him. I suffered multiple traumas, including multiple vertebral column fractures, many herniated discs, marrow and nerves compressions, a broken rib, etc. After accident I was in tremendous pain and Pitzi felt it. She used to come and lay on the affected areas and suffered along with me...

Everybody loved Pitzi. Unfortunately, nothing lasts forever! In the morning of 27 October 2019, at one year after my motorcycle accident and when I suppose she was at least 12 years old, Pitzi died of unclear reasons without being able to breath in the final phase. A few days before that, she stopped eating so I took her to the vets but they were unable to tell the cause, nor to do something to help her. I tried to feed her with special liquid food, help her how I could and I stayed with her till her last moments without being able to save her. I also didn't think she would die till her last hours, as the vets haven't told me she was in such danger. She died like a warrior, without showing any signs of weakness: no crying, no nothing, she just looked sad but grateful that I am with her - though I could feel all the pain she went through... R.I.P. Pitzi, you've had a heart worthy of a great warrior!

Pitzi's Ancestry:

When Pitzi was still alive and with me, I knew about her ancestry as much as I knew about her date of birth: basically nothing! However, I was always amazed how much she resembles a Siberian Forest Cat of original color, which is tabby. That was more amazing taking into consideration that this breed had some very specific characteristics regarding the head, eyes, ears, fur, paws and tail, which are not common to the other cats and that they Love Water and Act More Like A Dog than a cat - which Pitzi did! It was only about 2 years after the Pitzi died, that I've found out that she was brought to that office by a lady who had such long hair cats at home.
